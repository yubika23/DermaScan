Skin Types & Conditions

Understanding your skin is the first step to protecting it.

Dry Skin

Feels tight, rough and flaky. Use gentle cleansers and heavy moisturisers. Avoid hot showers.

FlakyTightDull

Normal Skin

Well-balanced with small pores and few imperfections. Maintain with mild cleanser and SPF.

BalancedHealthy

Oily Skin

Excess sebum causes shine and enlarged pores. Use oil-free, non-comedogenic products.

ShinyPoresProne

Sensitive Skin

Easily irritated by products or weather. Use fragrance-free, hypoallergenic formulas only.

ReactiveRedness

Combination Skin

Oily T-zone, dry cheeks. Use targeted products for different facial zones.

MixedT-zone

Acne-Prone Skin

Prone to breakouts and clogged pores. Cleanse twice daily and use salicylic acid treatments.

BreakoutsComedones
Critical

Melanoma

The most dangerous skin cancer. Irregular borders, multiple colours, rapid growth. Requires immediate medical attention.

See doctor URGENTLY
High

Actinic Keratosis

Rough scaly patches from UV damage. Pre-cancerous lesions that may progress if untreated.

See doctor soon
High

Basal Cell Carcinoma

Most common skin cancer. Slow-growing, rarely metastasises but needs prompt dermatological treatment.

See doctor soon
Moderate

Melanocytic Nevus

Common moles. Most are harmless but monitor for ABCDE changes — asymmetry, border, colour, diameter, evolution.

Monitor & check-up
Low

Benign Keratosis

Harmless age-related growths. Can appear wart-like or scaly. Removal is cosmetic only.

Usually harmless
Low

Dermatofibroma

Firm benign nodule, often on legs. Dimples inward when pinched. No treatment usually needed.

Benign growth
Low

Vascular Lesion

Blood vessel abnormalities. Includes haemangiomas and spider veins. Usually benign, treatable with laser.

Usually benign
